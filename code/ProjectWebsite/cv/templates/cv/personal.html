Personal Statement

I have wanted to work in the field of Technology and Computer Science since starting a GCSE in Computer Science at Bishop Challoner Catholic College. I discovered a hidden talent for coding and software development right at the start of the course, receiving an introduction to coding in Python, which I quickly grew to appreciate and study outside of lessons, developing programs and projects for my own enjoyment. From this point onwards, I knew I wanted to work in the field of computer science. I continued with my studies at Bishop Challoner, while also considering where to go to further my understanding; I chose the University of Birmingham’s Computer Science course, specifically a degree apprenticeship with Vodafone UK, because it allowed me to pursue my interests and obtain valuable work experience at the same time.

I have completed the first 2 years of the course, where I have developed my understanding of other programming languages, such as Java, C and Haskell. Furthermore, through the modules, I have furthered my understanding of Artificial Intelligence, Functional programming and the fundamentals of software design. I also have a basic understanding of the aspects of business management from my Business Organisation and Management module, alongside an introduction to Financial Analysis – these skills I hope to develop further in my industrial year at Vodafone UK.

Before my time at the university, I developed my social interaction and team working skills by being part of the Scouting organisation, graduating through each level of scouting and making my way up to Explorers. Furthermore, I gained a more work-focused approach to teamwork during my team projects at the University, such as Artificial Intelligence, where we developed code that would navigate a small robot around a course, or Software Engineering, where we designed the outline and structure of an application to help the mental health of students.

Ideally I would see my future as being in a software development role in an organisation, working on larger external projects, whilst developing my software skill set.
